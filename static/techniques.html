Techniques

There are many hydroponic techniques (Kratky, DWC, NFT, ebb and flow, drip). Day to day, most systems run in one of two styles, or a mix of both.

Traditional

These systems are manually operated: check EC, pH, water level, temperature, humidity, and light hours, then adjust (nutrients, pH up/down, top-ups, lights).

  • Lowest cost; good for learning and small builds.
  • Works with simple PVC pipes, a pump, and a reservoir.
  • Needs a routine (for example, quick checks every 6 hours).

Smart

These systems are automated: sensors (EC, pH, temperature/humidity, water-level monitor, grow light) and a controller (for example, an ESP32). The system can manage dosing and top-ups, switch fans and lights, and send notifications to a phone.

  • More stable results with full automation.
  • Suited to heat swings and busy schedules.

Hybrid

These systems combine both approaches: for example, manual EC/pH with automated lighting, or manual checks with water-level monitoring. The level of automation depends on the user.

  • Cost-effective.
  • Flexible; less constant supervision.

What to monitor

EC pH Water level Temperature Humidity Light hours

Keep EC and pH in the ideal range for the crop, keep roots oxygenated, and meet the daily light requirement.
