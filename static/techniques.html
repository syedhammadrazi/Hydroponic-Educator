Techniques

There are many different types of techniques (Kratky, DWC, NFT, Ebb & Flow, & Drip). Day-to-day, most of them run in one of two styles — or in a mix of them.

Traditional

These systems are manually operated: check EC, pH, water level, temperature, humidity, and light hours, then adjust (nutrients, pH up/down, top-ups, lights).

  • Lowest cost, great for learning and small builds.
  • Works with simple PVC pipes + pump + reservoir.
  • Requires a routine (e.g., quick checks every 6 hours).

Smart

These systems are automatically operated: sensors (EC, pH, temp/humidity, water level monitor, grow light) and a controller (e.g., ESP32). It can automate dosing/top-ups, switch fans/lights, and notify on smartphone.

  • Steadier results; fully automated.
  • Good for heat swings and busy schedules.

Hybrid

These systems are a mix of both: manual EC/pH, but automated lighting; or manual checks with water-level monitoring. It depends on what level of automation the user wants.

  • Cost effective
  • Provides flexibility; less babysitting

What to monitor

EC pH Water level Temperature Humidity Light hours

Keep the EC/pH in ideal range depending on the crop, keep the roots oxygenated, and meet the daily light hours.
