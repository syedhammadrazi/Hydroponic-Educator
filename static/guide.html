How the Simulator Works

A 2-minute tour of what you control, why it matters, and how to get great results.

Quick Guide

  1. Pick your city, month, and crop: This loads the local climate (temperature, humidity & daylight) for the city in that month, and the crop’s ideal ranges for electrical conductivity, pH, temperature, humidity and light requirements.
  2. Press Start: To launch the simulation.
  3. Respond to prompts: If EC, pH, humidity, temperature, water, or light hours changes (goes out of the ideal ranges for the crop), the user gets a notification in the actions required tab, and one-tap action is required by the user.
  4. Live Dashboard: The dashboard tab shows the all the necessary infomartion to the user such as; day, hour, stage, water level, EC level, & pH level etc.
  5. Educational Feedback: If the user doesn't respond to a warning, it will deduct the crop's health, which will impact the overall yield. The users get an acknowledgment in the feedback tab if they respond to the warning, and a suggestive feedback if they miss a warning to educate them that each warning missed impacts the health of the crop.

Learning Outcomes

  • Water level — keep the reservoir topped so roots never dry.
  • EC (nutrient strength) — normalize to keep feeding steady.
  • pH — drift happens; bring it back inside the safe band.
  • Temperature — cool/heat by moving to shade/inside or sunlight.
  • Humidity — mist to raise; dehumidify to lower.
  • Light hours — meet the crop’s daily requirement using sun + grow light.

Control Panel

  • Left Controls: There are four controls on the left side (Light, EC normalisation, pH normalisation, Cooling), each of them can be tapped once to respond to the warnings.
  • Right Controls: There are four controls on the right side (Refill Water, Dehumidify, Spray Water, Heating), each of them can be tapped once to respond to the warnings.

Prompts, Health & Yield

  • Prompts appear only when an action is needed (e.g., “Normalize pH”).
  • Missed prompts applies a small health penalty (teaches urgency).
  • Finish: when the crop reaches its final stage, the user gets a yield estimate based on the final health.

Scenario — Cherry Tomato in Karachi in the month of July

Karachi’s July is warm and humid. This Scenario shows how the simulator helps the user in understanding how to manage crops in a hydroponics system: managing the heat/humidity.

What to expect

  • Dehumidify sometimes: muggy days can push humidity above comfort; use “Dehumidify.”
  • Cool the crop: if temperature exceeds comfort; use "Cooling".

Limitations & Design Choices

  • Not the most realistic simulator: It’s intentionally simple so it’s accessible to everyone in urban Pakistan—educated or not, English- or Urdu-speaking.
  • Learning-first: The goal is to teach core habits—refill water, normalize EC/pH, manage light & temperature and their impacts on the crop's health without overwhelming the user.
  • No real hardware: This is a learning simulator only; it has no physical components. In the future it can be adapted to work with real sensors/actuators (e.g., ESP32) with software changes.
  • No exact physics: It uses simple equations to approximate behavior: EC gradually decreases, pH drifts, water level drops, and temperature/humidity fluctuate. It doesn’t mimic real life exactly—just gives a realistic “flavor.”
  • Future-ready: Later we can add richer crop models, dosing algorithms, real weather APIs, and device integrations to make it more advanced and realistic.
