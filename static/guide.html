How the Simulator Works

A short overview of what you control, why it matters, and how to get good results.

Quick Guide

  1. Pick your city, month, and crop: This loads the local climate (temperature, humidity, daylight hours) for that city and month, and the crop’s ideal ranges for electrical conductivity (EC), pH, temperature, humidity, and light.
  2. Press Start: The simulation begins.
  3. Respond to prompts: When EC, pH, humidity, temperature, water level, or light hours move outside the ideal range for the crop, a warning appears in the “Actions Required” area. Each warning can be handled with a single tap on the relevant control.
  4. Live dashboard: The dashboard shows the main values at a glance: day, hour, growth stage, water level, EC, pH, and other key indicators.
  5. Educational feedback: Ignoring a warning reduces the plant’s health and lowers the final yield. Responding in time is acknowledged, and missing a warning triggers a short explanation so you see how each decision affects the crop.

Learning Outcomes

  • Water level — keep the reservoir topped so roots do not dry out.
  • EC (nutrient strength) — normalise EC to keep feeding steady.
  • pH — pH naturally drifts; bring it back inside the safe band.
  • Temperature — manage heat or cold by moving to shade/indoors or sunlight.
  • Humidity — mist to raise humidity; dehumidify to lower it.
  • Light hours — meet the crop’s daily requirement using sun and grow light.

Control Panel

  • Left controls: Four controls on the left (Light, EC normalisation, pH normalisation, Cooling). Each can be tapped once to respond to a warning.
  • Right controls: Four controls on the right (Refill Water, Dehumidify, Spray Water, Heating), also used with a single tap to handle warnings.

Prompts, Health, and Yield

  • Prompts appear only when an action is needed (for example, “Normalise pH”).
  • Missed prompts apply a small health penalty to highlight the importance of timely action.
  • Finish: When the crop reaches its final stage, the simulator shows an estimated yield based on the final health.

Scenario — Cherry tomato in Karachi (July)

Karachi’s July is warm and humid. This scenario shows how the simulator helps you understand heat and humidity management in a hydroponic system.

What to expect

  • Dehumidify sometimes: muggy days can push humidity above comfort; use “Dehumidify” when prompted.
  • Cool the crop: if temperature rises above the comfort range, use “Cooling.”

Limitations and Design Choices

  • Not a highly detailed simulator: It is intentionally simple so it is accessible to a wide range of users in urban Pakistan—regardless of education level or language.
  • Learning first: The focus is on core habits: refill water, normalise EC and pH, manage light and temperature, and see how these affect plant health, without overwhelming the user.
  • No physical hardware: This is a learning tool only; it does not connect to real sensors or pumps. In future it could be extended to work with hardware (for example, ESP32-based systems).
  • Simplified behaviour: It uses basic equations to approximate trends: EC gradually drops, pH drifts, water level falls, and temperature/humidity shift. The aim is a realistic “feel,” not perfect physical accuracy.
  • Room to grow: Later versions could include richer crop models, dosing logic, real weather data, and device integration.
